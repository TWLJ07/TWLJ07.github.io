About Me

I build things that move, sense, and respond.

I’m drawn to mechatronics because it sits at the intersection of mechanics, electronics, and software. My goal is to create systems that are intuitive to use, robust in the real world, and elegant under the hood.

What I focus on

I like working on robotics, automation, and embedded systems where small design choices affect reliability, latency, and user experience. I’m especially interested in control systems, sensor fusion, and hardware-software integration.

How I work

I approach projects like engineering problems: define constraints, prototype quickly, test often, and refine based on evidence. I’m comfortable collaborating in multidisciplinary teams and communicating technical details clearly.
